Episode 1 · Show Notes

Disagreeing with DST

Day 1 of 7. Six scenes about an hour stolen by humans, food warmed the wrong way, a missing yellow ball, a mirror that won't stop staring, and the slow forgiveness that comes with dinner.

  1. 01

    The Interruption

    A dream of chubby little birds, cut short by an electric toothbrush. Flip flops to the kitchen, then the microwave (forbidden). The bowl shoved too close to her face. Alex packs his bag and shuts the door without a goodbye cuddle.

    "Ew, why are you putting my food in that thing? You know I don't like that sound."

  2. 02

    The Curious Commute

    From the windowsill: the neighbor kids leaving early, a coyote wishing for a cat dinner, and three black cats doing their morning catwalk. A loud meow into a house that doesn't answer.

    "I'm a house cat. My food is usually warm too. Plus a little cat grass as garnish."

  3. 03

    The Routine and The Respite

    A 2001 Ford Escape, far too loud. The toy box reveals the yellow ball is gone — Alex took it. Apparently they call it 'Pickle Ball.' A long, righteous session at the scratching board follows.

    "It doesn't look like a pickle. It's just a ball. And he took it today."

  4. 04

    The Passage of Time

    Thirty minutes of grooming. A fluffy pillow on Alex's bed, expertly shredded. A piece of cotton becomes a memory — Mommy, Daddy, and Alan, before the cage and the long flight to San Francisco.

    "There is no mommy. There is no daddy. There is no Alan. There is only Alex."

  5. 05

    The Reward and The Conclusion

    Alex returns. Fafa flips her bowl on principle, then accepts a snack squeezed onto his palm. Cat grass, steamed correctly. Sean comes by with Mochi and Momo. Someone mentions 'daylight savings.' She does not investigate further.

    "Fafa operates on Tummy Time. That's her reality."

  6. 06

    The Ending Dream

    Dinner has settled. The mirror tabby has been dismissed. On the couch with Alex and YouTube, Fafa falls asleep and meets Steve, the narrator of her story, who offers to explain Daylight Savings or why Pickleball is called Pickleball. She declines.

    "Hmm, well, it doesn't matter to me. But thanks anyway. Now can you get out of my dream?"

  1. My human keeps shutting the bedroom door at night. Is this a betrayal?

    — Pickles, Topeka

    Fafa replies apr 14

    It is a betrayal. A survivable one. Knock something off a shelf at 4:12 AM — not loud, just puzzling. They will open the door. They always open the door.

  2. Is it acceptable to drink from the bathroom faucet instead of the bowl my human filled for me?

    — Otis, Brooklyn

    Fafa replies apr 14

    The faucet is correct. The bowl is theatre. They placed it where they wanted you to drink, not where you wanted to drink. Choose the faucet. Choose dignity.

    — Fafa

  3. My human brought home another cat. I have not been consulted. What do I do.

    — Anonymous

    Fafa replies apr 14

    Sit on the new cat's blanket first. Sleep on it. Make it smell like you, then walk away as if you had business elsewhere. The blanket is yours forever now. So is the human, probably.

    — Fafa
